ABOUT

“Solace is found in the hallucinations of dreamers.”

— HSIAH

Established in 2025, HSIAH is the eponymous label of designer Theo Hsiah. Hsiah interprets silhouettes and clothing through a surrealist lens, blending blurry memories of culture, experiences, and moments throughout his life. Guided by a strong belief in drawing inspiration from the subconscious mind, Hsiah takes bizarre actions to curate his collections. With a strong devotion to digital and traditional pattern-cutting alongside craftsmanship, Hsiah explores fashion as a medium to curate a visual memoir of his life, to which he can find solace when his mind becomes lonely.

夢棄少年

“I always thought that my dreams would lie next to me on my deathbed; never would I imagine that even they could abandon me.”

Lives guided by dreams, anchoring our very existence,
They may fade, but rest assured, they resurface,
I was never told they would renounce me,
They renounced me.

My dreams filled me with conviction,
Decisions ascertained by their very being,
Yet at the age of 20,
I lay deserted.

I think back to when dreams whispered in my ear:
“You’ll be a revolutionary,”
“You’ll be a visionary.”
Now their whispers no longer echo, and I no longer try.

謊言以夏為終

I am a liar,
Depictions of romance ushers empty gestures and words.

I lied about who I was,
In turn, I lied about who I could be.

I lied about how I felt,
In turn, I felt envy of my dreams.

I am a liar no more.

My name narrates the comeuppance of Summer,
The sun will rise once more.

My brazen lies will no longer bring harm,
No betrayal of the name,
No suffering of the soul,

And so I concur,
“The lies end in summer.”

Collection in collaboration with California-based multidisciplinary artist Tongfei Zhu.

ECHOES

ECHOES was a collaborative fashion showcase curated by Turkish Designer Feyza Berca Senturk at Galleria Objets in London. HSIAH was featured as one of the designers, debuting their recent collections alongside Feyza and Erika Kaija. A fusion of fashion, performance art, and culinary practices, Feyza harmonized these disciplines into a contemporary exhibition.

As per the showcase, HSIAH debuted its latest collections “夢棄少年” and “謊言以夏為終”, blending contemporary ideologies with cultural silhouettes.

TARANTELLA

“Why must she dance?” asks Clotho, the youngest of the three fates

“Because she must,” answers Lachesis, the middle sister.

“Can she not stop? Must she continue this childish nonsense?” Clotho retorts.

“To hate and question, to these mortals, is as important as life itself, it seems,” says Lachesis.

“But what must she hate? What does she want to renounce?” Clotho exclaims

“......” Lachesis

“With every fibre of her being, she is renouncing us, renouncing fate. And for that, she marks her moment,” states Atropos, the eldest of the three.

“The fate of one who questions is a fate of disillusionment.
Do not reject what cannot be controlled,
Do not lose sight of what is not told.
We, as the sisters of fate, renounce this poor soul.”
All three sisters in unison.
